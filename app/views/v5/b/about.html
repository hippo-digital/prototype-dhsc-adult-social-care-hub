About adult social care

People working in adult social care help people who draw on care to be able to live their lives. The care may involve: 

  • personal care like washing and dressing
  • staying active in their communities
  • support with everyday life 
  • supporting people to live independently

This work can happen in different places such as:

  • people’s own homes
  • residential settings like care or nursing homes
  • supported independent living

People usually contact their local authority for short-term or long-term care. Local authorities assess the needs and later do a financial needs assessment which calculates how much of the care is paid for buy the local authority and how much by the person. 

  • Adult social care values

    Working in adult social care in England requires you to hold a set of values that were developed with people from the sector. These are the same across all roles in adult social care. You should have these values if you want to work in adult social care.

  • Career progression in adult social care

    If you are interested in a career in care you can take a look at our career pathways. They will help you to figure out where you would start in your career and the skills you’ll need to apply for a role in adult social care.
