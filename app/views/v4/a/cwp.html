Career pathways in adult social care

The role categories:

  • Role category A

    Entry-level roles

    People working in adult social care help people who draw on care to be able to live their lives. These roles are the starting point for a career in adult social care.

  • Role category B

    Care or support workers

    A care or support worker directly helps people who draw on care to be able to live their lives. This can be help with personal care, finances, or anything that helps a person live their life.

  • Role category C

    Supervisors or leaders

    A supervisor or leader is a team leader or manages a team of care workers. This can be a care or support worker or someone from another sector who is now working in adult social care.

  • Role category D

    Practice leaders

    A practice leader has experience in a specialism like autism or dementia and leads how to deliver specialist care. They are an expert in their chosen specialism.

How it works

The career pathways are designed to help people understand career options in adult social care. It helps you see where you are in your career and directly links to courses and training.

It contains information about the different job role categories in adult social care. This includes:

  • what you need to start a job role such as skills or experience
  • what each job role should do and the experience or skills that are needed to do it
  • the different options to develop a career in each job role
  • what training is relevant for each job role

It also links workers to courses and training.

Manage your career with the Skills Record

The Skills Record keeps all your training and practical experience in one place. You need to create an account to do this. 

You can see any courses, training or practical experience you have done to develop your career in adult social care. Your employer will also be able to see this. 

This includes:

  • everything you have done to progress your career in adult social care
  • what training you have already done and when you may need to update it
  • the type of training you have done so that you don't repeat training if you move to a new organisation
  • letting you know where you are in your career

Managers can also endorse skills and practical experience and you can request an endorsement from them.

Training providers can add what training you have done directly into the Skills Record.

Do training approved and funded by the government

You can find approved training providers and approved courses and training across the UK with a variety of learning options such as online or in person. 

DHSC does checks on training providers as well as the courses and training they do. It only approves those that meets their standards. 

If you start working in adult social care your employer can pay for your training or courses and then apply for a refund.
